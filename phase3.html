Phase 3 — Independence
Phase 1 taught you what to do. Phase 2 made you do it on paper, every day, until the habits became automatic. Phase 3 is where it becomes real money. Real wins. Real losses. Real psychology. The structure tightens. The size starts small. The mentor stays close, then steps back. Your job is to graduate from this phase as a trader who can survive without us.
Before you trade one share live: you have completed Phase 2 with all 8 graduation criteria met. Your mentor has approved you for Phase 3. Your live trading account is funded with capital you can afford to lose. You have read every section of this page in full. If any of those is not true, return to where you came from — Phase 3 is not the place to take shortcuts.

12-Week Live Capital Transition
Four phases over 12 weeks, ramping from micro size to full size. Each phase has a goal, success criteria, and a failure signal. The mentor decides when you advance — meeting numerical criteria is necessary but not sufficient.
+1% Per Qualifying Week
After completing the transition protocol (Tab 1), the size scaling formula governs further growth. Size grows by +1% only when you EARN it — meaning every one of the 4 gates was met for the previous week. Failing a gate triggers a 5% pullback. The system compounds slowly so your psychology grows with the size.
Live Trading Hard Rules — Tightened From Phase 2
Real money requires tighter rules than paper. Limits move from 1.5% daily / 3% weekly / 0.5% per trade in Phase 2 to 2% / 5% / 1% in Phase 3. Three new rules also activate (3-loss walk-away, daily-limit cooldown, drawdown mentor call) plus the cash-up rule for monthly profits.
When Things Go Wrong — Pre-Written Protocols
Every protocol on this tab exists because real traders have lost real money panicking in real-time. The protocols work because they remove decisions from the moment of crisis — you decide once, calmly, in advance. When the crisis hits, you execute the protocol. No thinking required.
8 Requirements for Graduation
Eight criteria stand between you and graduation from the program. Six are numerical and verifiable. Two are subjective psychological demonstrations. The mentor exit interview is the final gate — and the mentor's decision is final, regardless of numerical compliance.
Daily Capital Tracker
Log every trading day — including no-trade days. The streak of consecutive logged days is itself a graduation criterion. Aggregates calculate automatically from your daily entries. Be honest about violations; the tracker exists to help you graduate, not to look good.
